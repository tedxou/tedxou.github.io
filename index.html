ABOUT
TED

TED Conferences LLC (Technology, Entertainment, Design) is an American media organization that posts talks online for free distribution under the slogan "ideas worth spreading". TED was conceived by Richard Saul Wurman in February 1984[5] as a conference; it has been held annually since 1990.[6] TED's early emphasis was on technology and design, consistent with its Silicon Valley origins. It has since broadened its perspective to include talks on many scientific, cultural, political, and academic topics.[7] It is owned and curated by Chris Anderson, a British-American businessman, through the Sapling Foundation.[1][8]

ABOUT
TEDx

TEDx are independent events similar to TED in presentation. They can be organized by anyone who obtains a free license from TED, and agrees to follow certain principles.[75] TEDx events are required to be non-profit, but organizers may use an admission fee or commercial sponsorship to cover costs.[76] Speakers are not paid and must also relinquish the copyrights to their materials, which TED may edit and distribute under a Creative Commons license.[77]

As of January 2014, the TEDxTalks library contained some 30,000 films and presentations from more than 130 countries.[78][79] As of October 2017, the TEDx archive surpassed 100,000 talks.[80] In March 2013, eight TEDx events were organised every day; raised up from five in June 2012, the previous year, in 133 countries.[81][82] TEDx presentations may include live performances, which are catalogued in the TEDx Music Project.[83]

TEDx OSMANIA

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TEDTalks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized
